# TFT Set 17 — Glossary / Slang

Term: Slam
Definition: To immediately build and equip a completed item onto a unit rather than holding the components. Used when you want to use an item's stats right away, especially early in the game.
Example: "Slam a Deathblade on your carry if you hit B.F. Sword and a second component early."

---

Term: Roll / Roll Down
Definition: To spend gold refreshing the shop in order to find specific units. "Hard rolling" means spending most or all of your gold to find a key unit.

---

Term: Econ
Definition: Short for economy — refers to managing your gold effectively, usually by saving gold to earn interest (1 gold per 10 gold saved, up to 5 gold/round).

---

Term: BIS
Definition: Short for "Best in Slot", meaning very optimal or good, ideally the best.  S tier

---

Term: WIS
Definition: Short for "Worst in Slot", meaning very bad.  The worst outcome or D/F tier

---

Term: Tempo
Definition: The speed or pace of the game state

---

Term: High Tempo
Definition: Slamming items, rolling down, spending econ, doing actions that will increase the relative strength of lobby instantly

-- 

Term: Low Tempo
Definition: Saving econ for better interest, saving components for better items, essentially sacrificing current strength for a relatively stronger late game
